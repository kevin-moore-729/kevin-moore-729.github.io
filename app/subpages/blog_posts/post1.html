A Quick Foreword

Jun 1, 2018

48 hours ago, I was in the Dublin Airport. I had never heard of Gulp or Bootstrap or Node. I hadn’t eaten an Oreo in years. It’s amazing how things can change in just two days time. This summer I am participating in a Web Developer Bootcamp, dedicating to preparing people from various career paths for a new career in web and software development.

2 years ago, I was preparing for graduate school. I had just completed my bachelor’s degree and was headed to a good school on scholarship. I had never heard of Command Line or HTML or CSS. I only drank one cup of coffee every day. It’s amazing how things can change in 2 years time.

I never envisioned myself having any interest in programming as a career. I had always been a humanities kid. However, 2 years ago, I realized that the career path I had prepared for myself was one I was defaulting into. I was continuing to do what I had always done rather than considering if it was actually something I wanted to do. When I took the time to reflect on my goals in life, and the kind of career that I would need to meet them, web development became an incredibly attractive option.

Even though I had found a new career candidate, I dismissed it as impossible. I had no experience, nor the time for 3+ years of school to get a CS degree. At the encouragement of a friend who had completed the program, I looked up the Web Development Bootcamp at Awesome Inc. It promised to deliver everything I was looking for, and I was completely sold. When Awesome Inc offered me a spot in their summer bootcamp, I readily accepted.

Like I said, I was in a different country only 48 hours ago. My travel plans forced me to miss the first week and a half of the class. When the entire program is only 12 weeks, that’s a pretty substantial absence. Therefore, my first display was a crash course of a crash course. I don’t know if I ever had to take notes in my college career as fast as I had to take them here. In one day we must have covered what would take a month in a traditional classroom setting. We considered the power of CSS selectors, the convenience of tooling packages like Bootstrap and Sass, and the programming complexity of a simple calculator. In that time, I also had at least 4 Oreos. At least. After 8 hours of intensive study, I went home and tried to review more of what I had missed. I could feel the fatigue setting in, but every small breakthrough or connection I made pushed me forward. When I finally went to sleep, I was excited to do it again the next day.

All this to say, the content of this blog will be from the perspective of someone who is experiencing programming for the first time. I never expected to be here. This program, and the career that hopefully follows it, were never in my mind a few short years ago. But, after just 1 day, I am certainly thankful for how things can change in a short time.
